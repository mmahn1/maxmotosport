History of Max MotoSport

Max MotoSport was founded in 2006 as a family business, started by passionate motorcycle enthusiast Edvard Mahnič. After years of experience in the motorcycle industry, Edi decided to make his dream of a specialized Italian motorcycle shop come true.

Our shop started as a small workshop with a few motorcycles, but thanks to our dedication to quality and passion for motorcycles, we quickly gained a loyal customer base. In 2010, we became the official representative for the prestigious Ducati brand in Slovenia, which was an important milestone in our story.

Today, Max MotoSport is not just a shop, but a community of motorcycle enthusiasts. In addition to selling new Ducati motorcycles, we offer service, accessories and Ducati fashion clothing. We also organize regular meetings, trips and events for Ducati motorcycle owners.

Our philosophy is based on a personal approach to customers, technical knowledge and an unwavering passion for motorcycling. We strive to provide every visitor with an authentic experience of Italian motorcycle culture and to inspire them with the legendary Ducati brand.

History of Ducati

Ducati was founded in 1926 in Bologna, Italy, initially as a manufacturer of radio components. It was founded by brothers Adriano and Marcello Ducati. After the factory was bombed during World War II, the company switched to producing bicycle engines.

The first Ducati motorcycle, the little Cucciolo (Italian for "youngster"), appeared in 1946. It was essentially an engine that could be attached to a bicycle. The first real Ducati motorcycles began to be produced in the 1950s.

Legendary engineer Fabio Taglioni joined the company in 1954 and created the revolutionary Desmodromic valve system that became the trademark of Ducati engines. This technology allowed for higher engine speeds and more power, and is still one of the hallmarks of Ducati motorcycles today.

In the 1970s, Ducati developed its signature L-twin engine, with the cylinders arranged at a 90-degree angle. This design provided better balance and became synonymous with the sound and feel of a true Ducati motorcycle.

Over the decades, Ducati has become famous for its sports and racing motorcycles, especially with models such as the 916, Monster, Panigale and Multistrada. The company has successfully competed in various motorcycle championships, including MotoGP and Superbike, where they have won numerous world championship titles.

In 2012, Ducati was acquired by Audi AG, part of the Volkswagen Group. Under the new management, Ducati continued to develop innovative motorcycles that combine Italian design, technological advancement and racing DNA.

Today, Ducati is considered one of the most prestigious motorcycle manufacturers in the world, known for its high-performance models, innovative technological solutions and timeless design that combines tradition and modernity.
